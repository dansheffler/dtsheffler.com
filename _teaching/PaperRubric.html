Final Paper Rubric

Category  and Weight Nonexistent  = 0% Bad  = 50% D  = 65% C  = 75% B  = 85% A  = 95% Impressive  = 100%

Style
20%

Very difficult to read and follow train of thought because of grammar, spelling, or formatting.

Consistently poor grammar, spelling, OR formatting.

Several noticeable errors. Sloppy. Appears not to have been read over.

A few noticeable errors. Could have used further proofreading.

No noticeable grammar, spelling, or formatting errors.

Exceptionally well written. Impresses me with its writing quality.

Content
30%

Student does not write about a suitable issue.

Very difficult to understand what the student is arguing.

Points are altogether absent OR paper fails to follow instructions by not providing reasons in support of a thesis. (Book-report style papers fall into this category.)

Only cursory development of ideas OR weak connection between thesis and supporting reasons

A few items that are under-developed, OR a general but minor lack of cohesion between thesis and supporting reasons

Developed thoughts, clear structure, and logical cohesion.

Impressively fleshed out. Very clearly organized. Logical progression.

Thinking
30%

Student does not express his own view at all.

Very difficult to understand the thought process that this student is going through.

Paper is mostly or entirely references to other people’s thinking OR student appears to have importantly misunderstood core concepts we have discussed in class. (Consistent misuse of technical terms falls into this category.)

Student goes through arguments but it appears that some key concepts are not understood OR student is simply explaining what other people think.

Some good critical thinking, but not throughout the paper.

Student is clearly thinking critically about the issues for himself and is not merely regurgitating.

Impressive thinking above and beyond what I would expect of an undergraduate student.

Length
20%

One paragraph or less.

Less than 50% of the word count.

50–75% of the word count.

Substantially short of the word count.

Either falls just a little short OR contains a good bit of fluff just to meet the word count.

Suitably meets the word count with little to no filler.

Perfect. This paper contains absolutely no filler and meets the length requirement by expressing its ideas at the right level of analysis to fit into the given word count
